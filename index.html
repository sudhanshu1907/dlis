Department of Library and Information Science

The Department of Library and Information Science (DLIS) in India has its roots in the pioneering work of Dr. S.R. Ranganathan, known as the "Father of Library Science in India." He introduced the Five Laws of Library Science and the Colon Classification system, shaping modern librarianship. The first formal library science course began in 1911 at Baroda, followed by the University of Madras in 1931 under Ranganathan's guidance. Today, DLIS programs across India continue to advance research, digital librarianship, and information management preserving his legacy.
